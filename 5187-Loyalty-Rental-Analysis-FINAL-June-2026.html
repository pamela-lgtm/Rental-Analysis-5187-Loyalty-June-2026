04

Why Pricing Lower Wins More

It feels counterintuitive to "leave money on the table," but in an oversupplied market the math runs the other way. Because you own this property free and clear, you have the rare freedom to price for speed — and speed is what protects your return.

The cost of chasing a higher number

Hold out for an extra $100/month in rent+$1,200 / year
One extra month vacant while you wait (at ~$2,395)−$2,395
Time to recover a single vacant month via that premium~24 months

Chasing an extra $100/month takes two full years of occupancy just to recover one month of vacancy. The high-priced units on your street have already been empty far longer than that.

  • The submarket is saturated. The CMA shows a wave of recently built 4-bedroom homes listed at aspirational rents the area isn't supporting — which is why so many sit 70, 113, even 127 days. Pricing with the leased comps, not the asking comps, sidesteps that trap entirely.
  • Location sets the ceiling. Crossprairie is convenient — minutes to the Turnpike, about 20 minutes to Lake Nona's Medical City — but it is not a Lake Nona address, and the rents reflect that. The market clears in the $2,400s, not the $2,700s.
  • Free and clear is your advantage. With no mortgage, there is no debt payment forcing a high number. Your only real cost is vacancy, so the smart move is to lease quickly to a strong tenant and start the income clock.
  • Speed compounds. Leasing in under 21 days instead of 60+ keeps roughly six to eight weeks of rent in your pocket and gets your tenant settled before the next wave of competing new construction hits the market.
